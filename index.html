Maryam Rezaie

Vancouver, Canada

I’m a computer science PhD candidate in Human-Computer Interaction and data visualization, at SFU, focused on a simple but often overlooked problem: making complex systems truly understandable.

My research, supervised by Prof. Sheelagh Carpendale, challenges the assumption that step-by-step guidance is the best way to onboard users. Instead, I study how people naturally make sense of unfamiliar interfaces, and I design interaction paradigms that support this process.

In my thesis, I introduce serendipitous explanations which is a new approach that provides embedded, in-situ explanations as users interact with interface elements, rather than guiding them through a fixed, predefined path. My contributions have been published in leading venues, including IEEE TVCG and ACM PACM HCI.

Recently, I interned at Autodesk, where I worked on visualizing uncertainty in AI to support more informed decision-making.
